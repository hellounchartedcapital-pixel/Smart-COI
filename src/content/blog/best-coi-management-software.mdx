## The Problem with Manual COI Management

If you manage commercial properties, you already know this pain: vendor insurance certificates piling up in email, a spreadsheet that is perpetually three weeks out of date, and the lingering fear that one of your contractors has been working with an expired policy for months.

Manual COI tracking works until it does not. For most teams, the breaking point is somewhere around 20 to 30 vendors. After that, the time spent collecting, reviewing, and chasing renewals starts competing with everything else on your plate. The real cost is not the hours — it is the liability gap that forms when a certificate slips through the cracks.

COI management software exists to close that gap. But the market ranges from enterprise platforms with six-figure contracts to simple trackers that barely improve on a spreadsheet. This post compares six options based on what actually matters to property managers: extraction accuracy, compliance automation, vendor communication, onboarding speed, and pricing transparency. For a broader overview of [COI tracking software](/coi-tracking-software) and what to look for, start there.

## How We Evaluated Each Platform

Every tool in this comparison was evaluated against the same five criteria:

**1. Data entry method.** How does the software get information off a certificate PDF and into the system? Manual data entry, optical character recognition (OCR), or AI-powered extraction? This single factor determines how much time you save per certificate.

**2. Compliance automation.** Does the system automatically check extracted data against your coverage requirements, or do you still need to compare numbers manually? Automatic compliance checking eliminates the most error-prone step in the process.

**3. Vendor communication.** Can the platform send automated reminders to vendors when certificates are expiring? Is there a self-service portal where vendors can upload directly without going through you?

**4. Onboarding speed.** How fast can you get your existing portfolio of vendors and certificates into the system? Hours, days, or weeks?

**5. Pricing transparency.** Can you see the price on the website, or do you need to sit through a demo and negotiate with a sales team?

## 2. Certificial

**What it does:** Certificial focuses on real-time insurance verification by connecting directly with insurance agents and carriers. Their model requires the insurance agent to issue certificates through the Certificial platform, which provides source verification — you know the certificate came from a verified agent, not a vendor who might submit an outdated or altered document.

**Best for:** Large enterprises that need real-time policy monitoring and fraud prevention, and whose vendor base is large enough to justify the onboarding effort.

**Pricing:** Not published. Requires a sales conversation to get pricing.

**Standout feature:** Source verification. Because certificates flow through the agent rather than the vendor, the data is verified at the source. Policy changes are reflected in real time rather than waiting for a new certificate to be issued.

**Honest limitations:** Enterprise pricing and sales process. Requires insurance agents to adopt the Certificial platform, which creates friction in the onboarding process. ## 4. myCOI

**What it does:** myCOI provides certificate management with compliance tracking, automated follow-ups, and a network of insurance agents who submit certificates directly into the system. Their focus is on building a large agent network to streamline the certificate collection process.

**Best for:** Construction, real estate, and transportation companies with large vendor networks (hundreds to thousands of vendors) where the agent network effect provides the most value.

**Standout feature:** A large existing network of insurance agents who are already familiar with the platform and can submit certificates on behalf of their clients without additional setup.

**Honest limitations:** Enterprise-focused with pricing that reflects that positioning. Reports of long onboarding timelines. The platform works best when your vendors' agents are already in the myCOI network — if they are not, you face the same onboarding friction as any other platform.

**Honest limitations:** Dated user interface compared to newer platforms. Enterprise pricing with long implementation timelines. The onboarding process can take weeks rather than hours. See our full [SmartCOI vs BCS](/compare/smartcoi-vs-bcs) comparison for specifics.

## 6. Spreadsheets (Excel / Google Sheets)

**What it does:** Manual COI tracking using columns for vendor name, coverage types, limits, expiration dates, and compliance status. Conditional formatting flags upcoming expirations. Files are stored in a shared folder.

**Best for:** Teams managing fewer than 20 vendors who cannot justify a software subscription or who are just getting started with formalized COI tracking.

**Pricing:** Free (the software is free — your time is not). Expect 15 to 20 hours per month on data entry, manual review, email follow-ups, and file management for a portfolio of 30+ vendors.

**Standout feature:** Complete flexibility. You can track whatever you want in whatever format you want with no learning curve.

**Honest limitations:** No data extraction — you read every certificate and type in the values manually. No automated compliance checking — you compare numbers in your head or with a calculator. No automated reminders — you send emails yourself. No audit trail — you trust that the spreadsheet is correct because you are the one maintaining it. One missed expiration can cost more than a decade of software subscriptions. For a deeper analysis, see [COI tracking software vs spreadsheets](/compare/smartcoi-vs-spreadsheets).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best COI management software?**

The best COI management software depends on your portfolio size and how you work. For mid-market property managers who want self-serve onboarding and transparent pricing, SmartCOI is purpose-built for that segment. For enterprises needing real-time policy verification, Certificial is the leader. For teams on Yardi or MRI, Jones offers the tightest integration.

**How much does COI tracking software cost?**

Pricing ranges from free (spreadsheets) to enterprise-level contracts that require a sales conversation. SmartCOI is the only platform in this comparison with published pricing — starting at $63/month (annual billing) for up to 50 certificates, with a 14-day free trial and no credit card required.

**Can COI management software read PDF certificates automatically?**

Some platforms use AI or OCR to extract data from certificate PDFs. SmartCOI uses AI trained on ACORD certificate formats to extract coverage types, limits, dates, carriers, and named entities automatically. OCR-based tools (myCOI, BCS) can read text from PDFs but may struggle with formatting variations. Some platforms (Certificial) bypass PDF extraction entirely by requiring agents to submit data directly.

**What is the difference between COI tracking and COI management?**

COI tracking focuses on monitoring expiration dates and collecting certificates. COI management is broader — it includes compliance checking against your specific requirements, automated follow-ups when vendors are non-compliant, endorsement verification, and audit reporting. Most modern platforms handle both, but the distinction matters when evaluating features.
